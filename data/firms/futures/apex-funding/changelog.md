# Apex Trader Funding — Changelog

A history of notable changes to Apex Trader Funding's account structure, rules, and pricing. Most recent changes first.

## 2026-03 — 4.0 Update: elimination of monthly fees and product streamlining

Apex Trader Funding launched its "4.0" update in March 2026. Key changes:

- Monthly subscription fees eliminated; replaced with one-time evaluation purchase fees
- Activation fee structure introduced ($99 EOD / $79 Intraday) payable after passing evaluation
- Account lineup streamlined to four active sizes: $25K, $50K, $100K, $150K
- $250K and $300K account sizes discontinued for new purchases (legacy accounts continue under prior terms)
- No more payout denials tied to video review requirements
- Chart screenshot submission for payout approval removed

_Verified as of March 2026._

---

## 2024–2025 (approximate) — Intraday account type introduced

Apex added an Intraday (real-time trailing drawdown) evaluation type alongside the existing EOD accounts, offering traders a lower entry price in exchange for real-time trailing drawdown enforcement. This gave traders a choice between more intraday flexibility (EOD) and a lower upfront cost (Intraday).

Source: https://apextraderfunding.com

## 2021 — Company founded

Apex Trader Funding was founded in Austin, Texas in 2021. The firm launched with a monthly subscription evaluation model for CME futures traders, with the EOD trailing drawdown structure as its core risk management mechanism.
